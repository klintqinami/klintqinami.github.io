About

I am a machine learning systems engineer at Meta, working on compilers, frameworks, and kernels for in-house training and inference accelerators (MTIA), as part of the PyTorch AI framework organization. Previously, at Qualcomm, I worked on machine learning compilers, targetting wide-vector VLIW DSP accelerators. Before that, I was at Reservoir Labs, working on compiler toolchains for machine learning, polyhedral compilers, and software verification (before it was acquired by Qualcomm). I was briefly a Ph.D. student at Princeton, working on bias mitigation in machine learning. As an undergrad at Columbia, I worked on computer graphics, physics-based simulation, and geometry processing. I really like performance optimization in the context of large, practical systems.
